I am a PhD student in Social Statistics at the University of Manchester, UK. My main research interests revolve around Social Network Analysis and Computational Social Science. I aspire to apply advanced statistical methods to real-world problems and have versatile experience in harnessing time-series, spatial, textual and network data from several domains.

Before coming to Manchester, I worked as a consultant at the World Bank and as a senior analyst at the Russian Public Opinion Research Center. My projects at the World Bank involved the usage of novel data sources for education research. I exploited the Big Data of CVs posted on HeadHunter website for estimating the supply of skills in the labour market and utilised linked data from financial and administrative sources to inform policy decision-making processes.

I am also an active participant in Data Analytics competitions hosted on Kaggle and other platforms. Together with Ekaterina Melianova we took 2nd place (8,000$) in Kaggle ML & DS Survey competition and 1st place (10,000$) in DS4G - EIE competition organised by The Environmental Insights Explorer team at Google. Recently we became prize-winners in CDP - Unlocking Climate Solutions competition (25,000$) and COVID-19 Facebook challenge (30,000$) organised in partnership with Facebook Data For Good.

During my work and studies, I used a variety of programming languages such as Python, R, and JavaScript. I also have experience with database management using SQL, MongoDB, and Spark.

More detailed information can be found in my CV.
